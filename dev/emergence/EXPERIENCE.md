## The Lifecycle of a Thought

The experience of Project Emergence is not centered on editing.

It is centered on preserving and cultivating thought.

---

# Stage 1 — Expression

The writer begins expressing a thought.

Expression is completely editable.

Nothing has yet entered history.

The system minimizes every possible friction.

---

# Stage 2 — Seal → Emergence 🌱

Seal recognizes that an Expression has faithfully appeared.

At the moment of sealing,

the Expression ceases to exist.

An Emergence is born.

History begins here.

---

# Stage 3 — Descendant 🌿

The writer returns later.

Instead of editing the sealed thought, the writer creates a Descendant.

The Descendant inherits its ancestry while remaining fully independent.

---

# Stage 4 — Reflection 🌳

Multiple descendants begin revealing relationships.

Patterns emerge.

Contradictions become visible.

Reflection transforms isolated thoughts into understanding.

---

# Stage 6 — Publication 📖

Any node may be published.

Publishing selects one visible expression.

It never declares the genealogy complete.

---

# Continuous Evolution

Every published work remains capable of generating future descendants.

The genealogy never closes.

Only individual thoughts are sealed.

The living lineage continues indefinitely.

---

# Experience Principles

* Capture before refinement.
* Preserve before correction.
* Descend rather than overwrite.
* Reveal ancestry without overwhelming the writer.
* Keep the interface quieter than the mind.
* Make evolution visible only when it is useful.
* Treat writing as cultivation, not replacement.
